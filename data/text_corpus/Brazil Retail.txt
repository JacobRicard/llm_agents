Dear Secretary,

I hope this letter finds you in good health and high spirits. I am writing to you today to discuss the trade relations between our two nations, specifically in the realm of Retail goods. Over the past few years, our mutual trade has seen both significant successes and areas that require our attention for improvement.

Firstly, I would like to highlight the success we have achieved in the trade of Brazilian coffee and footwear. Our coffee exports to TGNSI have seen a steady increase over the years, with a growth rate of 15% annually. This is a testament to the high quality of Brazilian coffee and the discerning taste of TGNSI's citizens. Similarly, our footwear exports, particularly our eco-friendly and sustainable lines, have been well-received in TGNSI, contributing to a 20% increase in trade volume in this sector.

However, there are areas where we can work together to improve. The trade of Brazilian textiles and furniture to TGNSI has not been as robust as we would like. While the quality of these goods is high, we believe that there are opportunities to improve the trade balance. We propose a joint marketing campaign to increase awareness and demand for these products in TGNSI. Additionally, we suggest exploring the possibility of reducing tariffs on these goods to make them more competitive in your market.

Furthermore, we have noticed a decline in the quality of electronic goods imported from TGNSI. While we value the technological innovation that TGNSI brings, we believe that there is room for improvement in terms of product durability and after-sales service. We are confident that addressing these issues will lead to a significant increase in consumer satisfaction and, consequently, trade volume.

In conclusion, while we have made significant strides in our mutual trade, there is always room for improvement. We look forward to working closely with you to address these issues and further strengthen our trade relations. 

Thank you for your attention to these matters. I am confident that through our continued collaboration, we can achieve a mutually beneficial trade relationship that serves the interests of both our nations.

Yours sincerely,

Secretary of Commerce
Brazil
