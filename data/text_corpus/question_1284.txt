QUESTION:
The M-Technic Luxury Equipment option was offered on 1991 model year vehicles. It was a limited production option. One site suggests that about 100 were produced, while another website suggests that the number was between 140 and 250. Both website suggest that production began in April of 1991 (4/91); however, I personally have one built in early May of 1991 (5/91). In 1991 there was an option, S771A, that was the "full" or "real" M-Technic. It was available only on the cabriolet (convertible in American speak). However, there was also an M-Technic "appearance" package or S770A. The S771A option is "771 M Technic Luxusausstattung" (luxury equipment in english) or the 771 Luxury for short. According to e30cabrio.se the M-Technic Luxury Equipment option package in the United States included the following (translated from Swedish): Body - Face Lifted Body - M Technic II aerodynamics package without rear spoiler Colors - Brilliantrot [paint code: 308] and convertible top in Schwarz (Black) - Macaoblau metallic [250] and convertible top in Dunkelblau (Dark Blue) or Schwarz (Black) - Sterlingsilber metallic [244] and convertible top in Dunkelblau (Dark Blue) or Schwarz (Black) Interior/Upholstery - Anthrazit M Tex 0344 full leather upholstery (not clear if this is "full" or "extended") Equipment/Options - 288 Alloy Wheels Cross spoke 15x7 wheels (BBS euroweaves) - 339 Shadow Line (trim) - 350 Green Tinted windows with thermal power - 375/377 Electro-mechanical convertible top (automatic top) in Black or Dark Blue - 704 M Technic sports suspension Included in option 771: - M Technic II aerodynamics package without rear spoiler - BMW M Technic door sill plates - BMW M Technic badges on the grill and trunk lid - Alloy Wheels Cross spoke 15x7 - Delayed dimming of interior lighting - Lining the inside of the trunk lid - Leather steering wheel with airbag - Shifter knob and boot in leather (selector lever in leather on the automatic) Background I do not pretend to know everything about the M-Technic; however, what information has been published over recent years seems to be spotty at best. Since there are so few of these cars I thought it would be good to get an official thread pulled together to collect as much information as possible in a single place. I know there has been talk of a registry, but it doesn't seem to have ever materialized, so maybe we could do that here as well. I'll block off a few posts to capture data in the future and try to keep the original posts updated as people tack on more information (assuming that is allowed). From e30ic.com: In '91, the full M-Technic option added about $4,700 to the normal price. Available in Sterling silver, Brilliant red and Macau blue. Changes included: lowered suspension, full body kit, special leather/cloth interior, 15" wheels, M-Technic badges, M-Technic door sills, leather console, e-brake boot. M-Technic vs. "M-Technic appearance" The S770A option is "770 M Technic weiss/schwarz" (white/black in english) or the 770 Appearance for short. According to e30cabrio.se the M-Technic "appearance" option package in the United States included the following (translated from Swedish): Body - Face Lifted Body - M Technic II aerodynamics package without rear spoiler Colors - Alpinweiss [paint code: 218] and convertible top in dark brown with Lotusweiss leather (White) - Diamantschwarz metallic [181] and convertible top in Schwarz (Black) Interior/Upholstery - Nappa full leather interior in Schwarz (Black) 0318 or Lotus Weiss (White) 0462 Equipment/Options - 280 Alloy Wheels Cross spoke 14x6.5 wheels (BBS basket weave) in Alpine Weiss (Alpine White) 218 or black rims with Diamond Black metallic 181 - 350 Green Tinted windows with thermal power - 375/377 Electro-mechanical convertible top (automatic top) in Black or Dark Brown Included in option 770: - M Technic II aerodynamics package without rear spoiler - BMW M Technic door sill plates - Lining the inside of the trunk lid - Leather steering wheel with airbag - Shifter knob and boot in leather (selector lever in leather on the automatic) The M-Technic appearance package was about $1,600. [reserve for differences between the two versions] Interior: An M-Technic Luxury (S771A) will have leather wrapped interior, including leather door panels, leather center console, etc. According to a member of the bmwe30ic user group, "JM" mike_mulholland, "If you pull the rear seat out and look at the back their will be the factory stamp for the seat part number, and because its a limited edition and put together by hand, the engineer will have written the number in the series.....mine is #5......its the 5th mtechnic made in the series in 4/91....I used to also own a red one and it was #176 made in 6/91." bradnic chimes in to say, "This is the build order number for the car, and it's written on all the custom interior parts, either directly on the part by hand or with a sticker. On my former car the interior craftsman actually signed the console." Upgrades using stock parts: Trunk Lid Shocks One of the issues that the M-Technic has is trunk shock failure. The added weight of the trunk liner can cause the trunk shocks to failure prematurely. There is an interesting discussion of the trunk shock changes here. People with trunk spoilers often have the same sort of issue. It appeared that buying 2 of the older shocks will solve the problem; however it did not. Two 300nm shocks are way too much lift force and make the trunk lid not close properly. It appears we're doomed to replace 150nm shocks every few years.
