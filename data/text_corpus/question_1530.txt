QUESTION:
I have a 1989 ix sedan with 145K miles I am peeling back the layers of parts that are worn out. Struts look shot as the shock bumpers are crumbling and the boot are not attached. I am looking to restore the car to a mostly stock ride height but would not mind better performance. Any recommendations? Is it a good idea to replace the springs now too? Do I need to do front and rear at the same time? Looking to be efficient with my mechanics time not necessarily save money on parts. I plan to do all the control arms and bushings at the same time.

ANSWER 1 (id: 1530_0):
Springs dictate ride height, not shocks. If you are ok with the ride height, no need to replace the springs. For shocks you have OE, Bilstein HD, and Koni Yellows.
