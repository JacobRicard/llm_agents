After 12 years of sitting (plus however many more before being changed last), the timing belt has to be changed- to be safe, I'd do it before trying to start the motor.

ANSWER 5 (id: 1576_5):
Pour some ATF into the spark plug holes before you do the timing belt and let it sit there a while. Turn the motor over by hand a few times. Dump all old gas. Probably best to replace all the fuel lines. Bleed the brake lines and replace the brake rotors and pads. Do the timing belt, spark plugs, air filter, radiator, coolant, and battery, and then try to start it.
