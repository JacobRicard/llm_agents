ANSWER 1 (id: 161_0):
You should manual swap. Completely transforms the driving experience. Old automatic transmissions are dogs compared to modern offerings. My car was originally an M20B25 with an Auto box. I have used both a G260 and a ZF320 behind an M52B28. The G260 was out of a 1986 325E and mated up perfectly to the M20B25 using the original single-mass flywheel and clutch. Think I had to change the pilot bearing on the engine side. When I swapped to an M52B28, I bought a custom trans bracket to account for the difference in "twist" on the 24v engine. Gear ratios were good and it was plenty strong behind a bolt-on M52. I now have a ZF320 behind the same engine (again had to change the pilot bearing), as it is stronger for my turbocharged application. Unfortunately this trans has a 1:1 5th gear (no overdrive), so I needed to change the rear differential as well from a 3.73LSD to 2.73LSD to bring the cruising revs down.
