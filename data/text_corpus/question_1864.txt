QUESTION:
Hey everyone. Trying to diagnose internal fuel pump issue. I am trying to see if it is getting any power using a multimeter. Am i doing it right? Multimeter confirms 12v at the battery, testing the pump plug both with and without cranking and reads 0v all the time. Tried a bulb as well, nothing. Could it be another issue such as cps, ecu or something else? I thought the fuel pump(s) would be hot at all times with ignition on. Any help greatly appreciated Early 1987 325e with internal and external pumps. ​

ANSWER 1 (id: 1864_0):
Very very bad idea to be probing with the fuel pump out, fuel vapour can ignite if you create a spark. checked to see if the fuel pump relay has power output to the pump?

ANSWER 2 (id: 1864_1):
Pump will prime when key is first turned on for about 5 seconds, but will then shut off again until the engine is running. You can jump the fuel pump relay for testing if you want to keep those wires on.
