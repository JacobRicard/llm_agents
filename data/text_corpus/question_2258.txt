ANSWER 1 (id: 2258_0):
There are lots of options, actually- the M20 has the same bellhousing as the M4x, M5x series engines, so you can use any 5 speed from almost any later six or even DOHC 4 cylinder. There are lots of caveats, of course. Around here, the G260, the 'right' 5- speed is getting hard to find. So wherever it is that you're at, start shopping and see what you can find. The later transmissions need a custom shift platform (cut and weld, is all) if you're picky about where the lever ends up and so forth. The differential depends on the transmission- if you use a later 5- speed, then you're looking for a lower ratio (2.93- 3.23) version, as 5th gear is 1:1. If you use the M20 5 speed, the 3.64 in there works ok. If you get lucky and find an E46 6- speed, then you can go as high as a 4.11 or even 4.27 or 4.44 for lots of quick shifting. THEN you get to sort out the driveshaft. I tend to worry about that last, and budget $500, so that if I need to have one made, it's not a shocker.
