ANSWER 1 (id: 2354_0):
Go read in the m20 section. In short, putting an 885 head on a stock 2.7 bottom end is not a good idea. You may get a little more torque in some area, but it's a horrible combination of parts. Either have the crank ground and use the next oversize bearings, or look into building a 2.7i engine. Or call Bimmerheads and ask them for a suggestion.

ANSWER 2 (id: 2354_2):
Get the 2.7L 81 mm crank and 130 mm rods and combined with your m20b25 pistons. shove this into your m20b25 block but deck the block around ~2 mm so the piston doesn't sit so far down the bore when at TDC. Add an adjustable cam get and you have a decent CR 2.7L with mild cam and it would be a nice combo simply putting your cammed 885 onto an eta will lead to less improvement than people think otherwise just find a good m20b25 crank and reassemble your block or a good used short block
