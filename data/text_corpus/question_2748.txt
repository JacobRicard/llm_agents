ANSWER 1 (id: 2748_0):
What year is your car? The fuel gauge has 2 different configurations based on whether there is a crossover pipe between the 2 sides, which changed in (1988?) If there is, there is one 60 ohm sender that is directly connected to a 60 ohm gauge. If there is no crossover pipe, but instead a 'jet sucking pump' then there are 2 60 0hm senders, wired in SERIES, and then connected to a 120 ohm fuel gauge. If the gauges are scrambled, you get weird readings. If someone put a non- crossover tank into a crossover- tank car, you get very non- linear readings that fluctuate. there are wiring diagrams at http://www.wedophones.com/BMWManualsLead.htm hth t
