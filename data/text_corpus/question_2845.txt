QUESTION:
Good Morning, I am currently back in the market for another E30 after being away for awhile. I am going to go see an E30 today. It is a 1991 325ic. Supposedly, it has a rebuilt engine and transmission, new paint and new interior. From the pictures, it looks like a really nice vehicle but it does have an automatic transmission. The seller is asking 8500. With the assumption that everything is in good to great condition; how much does an automatic E30 with 199K miles go for these days? Vehicle Specs; -Rebuilt Engine -Rebuilt Automatic Transmission -Timing Belt replaced -Water Pump replaced -New front struts -New Motor Mounts -New Steering Rack -A/C works -Heated seats work -New convertible top -New interior

ANSWER 1 (id: 2845_1):
I would offer no more than $5000; $5500 if you really love it. You can buy 325ic's all day long in Florida for $5K in this condition. Other thoughts: New top and interior are a great bonus, assuming the work was done well. Do you want an automatic? If not, the swap will cost $800 to $1500 depending on how many used parts go in, and whether you do the work or not. I like all the work done, but does it need rear struts? New fuel lines, new cooling hoses (all of them, not just the radiator hoses), and vacuum lines are also in need of replacement if not already done. I would want to know who did the engine rebuild and how long ago. Same with the transmission. Original and working well are generally preferable to rebuilt. Not all rebuilds are the same. Market also prefers original paint in good condition over repaints.

ANSWER 2 (id: 2845_2):
I agree with everything mentioned already. E30 Vert's with auto is the "pipe and slippers" spec of e30's (nobody wants one as a "drivers car"). You can easily find one with lower mileage for the same price elsewhere.
