ANSWER 1 (id: 3022_0):
You should look in to that Brooklands book "E30 Restoration Bible." It's a few years old, but in the first chapter he discusses the disposition of the earliest examples. From what I can recall, many of the first ones produced in Germany where used by the press and ended up getting sold. I thought the same happened in the US. Now, there is many Grey market cars that came to the US before the official release of the E30. One of our racecars is being built on one of these grey market cars (a 316) that was too far gone to restore. It was actually built 04/83.. I used to have the vin plate and some parts from another '83 shell that was cut up and crushed as well.

And unless you have one of the first couple produced, a low serial number won't really affect it's value. What will, is keeping it low miles and original. Unless you perform tasteful mods, but even that is always debatable (think M50 swap vs M52.) And even then, there's nothing set in stone. Take touring's, before they hit the 25 year mark, you saw even rust buckets trading for 10k, now as more and more come over, the prices have come down and become a little more reasonable.
