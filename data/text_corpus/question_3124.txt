ANSWER 2 (id: 3124_6):
There's still a few cars here and there that aren't on the registry.. He tries to keep it up to date, but it's only one dude, and he has all models/builds to keep track of (which with the recent changes to them being produced on the BMW assembly line and pretty much becoming mass produced models now, it's a lot..) That being said, to find out it authenticity, you'd need to see a few things: -The Alpina VIN that Raul mentioned. -The correctly numbered plaque -If a Bucholoe built car, the stamping in the shock tower (Synter cars didn't have this.) -Also if it's a Bucholoe car, the crossed out BMW cowl VIN -The 4 digit stamping on the head -The stamping on the Engine block (Alpina engine series + car number) And even these days, people won't accept that as 100%. unless you have the build sheet. Either the original, or a reprint from Bucholoe. I've seen a lot of crazy stuff done to Alpina's, so even with all the painting and what not, doesn't rule it out as legit.
