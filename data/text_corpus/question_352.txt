Thanks for info. Thanks for the "E" number link. As to the 12 degree question, the M3's have less body roll-hence less camber gain. (IMO) the best improvement to an E30's handling is minimizing body roll with better(stiffer)swaybars, mounts, and links. Especially before stiffer springs.(/IMO) Link is to graphs of toe/camber changes per inch of wheel travel for the 12(M3) and 15(E30) degree arms. http://e30m3performance.com/tech_art...rves/index.htm From these changes you have to subtract body roll. Less body roll=less static camber.

ANSWER 6 (id: 352_7):
Originally posted by Jordan Guys, to clarify. He did that swap out of desperation. Not because its better or desirable, or anything of that nature. It does have some pretty good points but is in no way a bolt-in. Like: The Trailing arms have a 12degree sweep angle (15d for E30 & E36 ti, coupes & roadsters), this means the toe & camber don't change as much with suspension travel. I have also retained the "Dog-Bones" these further reduce the toe changes. The subframe is about twice the size, so if it every bends chances are the car is F*#ked, as are the trailing arms (which are substantially braced even compared to the M-Coupe ones). so not quite "desperation", more carefully assessing the pro's-con's of so much fabrication work, a 9" would be desperation :) Oh, and ask Saku, Kilander or Andreas what back-end they run in there +700hp monsters...
