ANSWER 1 (id: 3535_0):
As they say, "you get what you pay for." Applies here. Used parts online are always a gamble, so for peace of mind, to ease tuning issues, and to aid in eliminating potential failure points/fire hazards you should just pony up for new. Technically, they aren't counterfeit as far as the seller is concerned as they aren't branded and China has basically zero patent infringement protections. We could talk about international laws, and reasonable ties/conducting business, but why bother because China. So, if you wanted used ones, only buy those that look like quality parts and are hopefully branded. Edit: Since I didn't say it, the eBay ones look like cheap crap, just look for clean casting lines and good mold release marks when looking at important parts.
