ANSWER 1 (id: 78_0):
You’re going to have to do some electrical troubleshooting. I recommend you consult the ETM for your year and model of e30. Easiest test may be to substitute a known good hazard switch and see if that cures the issue. The solder traces on the instrument cluster going bad could also cause this type of problem, and are more difficult to troubleshoot.

ANSWER 4 (id: 78_3):
The switches on E30 cars are serviceable. Try popping it apart and look for fried contacts. Window switches, for instance will sometimes go on for years after a "failure". I clean them up and put a sparing amount of dielectric grease on the contacts. Can anyone here confirm that the hazard switch is serviceable?
