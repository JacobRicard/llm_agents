As you can see from the pic, water temp gauge is showing 50 deg C and oil temp 60ish deg C. Which is impossible I think. I just came back from a 45 min drive in 35 deg C air temp. ANSWER 1 (id: 850_0):
Whelp, on my screen, that's a tiny picture, but #3 is measuring the temperature of the water going to the radiator, NOT the temperature of the water in the engine. Under heavy load, that should read correctly, but under part load, it may read low. #2 I can't really see. The ECU reads oil temp at the filter housing- if you're measuring there, and your ECU says something different, do you have a 150c sender in there? #1 is measuring pressure in the top of the filter housing. #0 is that the ECU usually knows best! hth t
