ANSWER 2 (id: 882_1):
M52 with G240 from a 318is, so it has a slightly longer output shaft, E36 325i Manual driveshaft worked, probably too short for a 318i G240. G240 from a 318i will use a 320i Manual Driveshaft, you might find the manual 318is Driveshaft would fit, I found it was about 2cm too long to fit with my setup, but if you have the shorter G240 it might fit.

ANSWER 5 (id: 882_6):
Using a g240 with the M50 results in a distance of about 59 inches from face of guibo disk to face of diff. flange (4.10 unit), so the stock e30 318is driveshaft will not work. Even when the front half spline is pushed all the way into the rear half, the drive shaft measures about 61 inches total length, and the second problem is the mounting holes on the CSB bracket will not line up with the holes on the body. I am in the middle of M50 nv swap and using the car' stock g240 (318is) and I found that the front half of the stock drive shaft measures about 26.5 inches from the face of the guibo disk to the center of the center support bearing which is about 2.5 inches too long. I was able to find (local pic a part) a front half drive shaft from an e30 325i which measured about 24 inches from face of guibo to center of CSB, and that worked great.
